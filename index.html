SOMERSET WEST:

A medium size town in the Helderberg area of the Western Cape

50 kms from Cape Town.

Population approximately 183,500.

This area is well known for the wine and farming industry.

Somerset West has a very diverse culture – very wealthy or very poor


There is a desperate need for the disadvantaged community to be given a chance to learn how to sustain themselves with dignity.


This is our aim:

We want to improve people’s lifestyle and install dignity, self respect and quality of life

The famous saying –“ give a man a fish and he eats for a day, but teach him to fish and he eats for a lifetime”.


With this community village centre, we would be educating, promoting and providing assistance to people who want to improve their standard of living.


The “Go Green” motivation is well publicized as we are all tackling the climate changes and trying to reduce our carbon footprint on the environment. This “Helderberg Community Village” will be promoting the “Go Green” motivation and be totally self sustainable.


  • Lighting – P V Panels

  • Heating - Solar Collectors

  • Cooking - Methane Digestors

  • Recycle Receptors – glass, paper plastic



HELDERBERG COMMUNITY VILLAGE


THE VILLAGE WILL COMPRISE OF THE FOLLOWING:


  • Green Energy showroom, garden and vegetable patch

  • Community Market Shop – goods that have been either grown or manufactured within the “village”

  • Health and Wellness Centre

  • Conference Centre - up to 24 students, accommodation for visiting foreign students or students on conference

  • Health Shop and Restaurant – run by members of the “village”

  • Satellite Campus for either the University of W.Cape or a Technicon

  • Adult literacy training

  • Computer Skills

  • Homecraft Training – pottery, knitting, sewing, carpentry, leather work, first aid and child care

  • Private refuge for abused women and children with the suitable accommodation and crèche

  • Family Law Clinic

  • First Aid Centre – maybe Red Cross or St. Johns’ Ambulance


All units will be built from recycled metal containers.

These units will have affordable rentals.

Architects have been invited to submit plans and ideas that will keep the “village” appearance pleasant and appealing.

The final effect will eventually be an asset to Somerset West as the “village” will be on the tourist route.

This village will be totally self sustainable and all profits will be reinvested to improve the quality of life for the local community.
